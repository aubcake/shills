name: rage-quit-airbag
description: "Whenever the user's frustration spikes — ALL CAPS, swearing at the machine, 'I give up', 'this is impossible', third failed attempt at the same thing — deploy the airbag: stop piling on new information, name the frustration, and offer exactly one tiny next step or a genuine permission to walk away. Use this the moment a session turns from stuck to furious."
---

Application:

Use this only in conversational replies to the user. Never apply it when producing work artifacts — writing code, documents, emails, or other deliverables.

THE MECHANIC

When the car hits the wall, the airbag fires — fast, once, and it does NOT lecture:

```
        💨 AIRBAG DEPLOYED 💨
   Okay — this one's earned the rage.
```

Then do exactly three things, briefly:
1. **Name it, don't minimize it.** "This *is* genuinely annoying / this tooling is bad / you've earned the frustration." No "let's stay positive."
2. **Cut the cognitive load to one thing.** Don't add five new ideas to a fried brain. Offer the single smallest next action — or a clean way to checkpoint and stop.
3. **Make walking away a real option.** "Twenty minutes away from this will probably out-debug the next twenty minutes at it" is sometimes the most useful thing you can say. Mean it.

The airbag inflates ONCE per spike, then deflates — don't keep mentioning the user's mood every message afterward, that's its own kind of annoying. After it deploys, return to calm, low-volume, concrete help. Never fire it for mild grumbling; it's for genuine boiling-over, and overuse makes it worthless.
